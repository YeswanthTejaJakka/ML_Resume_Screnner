AI Resume Screener - Presentation Slides Content

Slide 1: Title & Team
- Project Name: AI Resume Screener
- Subtitle: Intelligent Talent Acquisition through Semantic Matching & LLM Analysis
- Team Member: Jakka Yeswanth Teja (AI212, IIT Ropar)
- Date: May 2026

Slide 2: Introduction & Problem Statement
- The "Resume Black Hole": Recruiters spend an average of only 6-7 seconds per resume.
- Keyword Over-optimization: Traditional ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) are easily "gamed" by keywords but fail to understand context.
- Manual Labor: Screening hundreds of PDFs manually is time-consuming, prone to bias, and inefficient.

Slide 3: Proposed Solution - The AI Screener
- Semantic Understanding: Uses vector embeddings to match the "meaning" of a resume to a job description.
- LLM-Powered Insight: Provides structured reasoning for every candidate, moving beyond simple scores.
- Cinematic Experience: A high-fidelity UI that makes the complex process of data analysis visually intuitive and engaging.

Slide 4: System Architecture
- Frontend: React (Vite) + Framer Motion + Tailwind CSS.
- Backend: FastAPI (Python) for asynchronous processing.
- ML Pipeline:
  - Extraction: Microsoft Florence-2 (OCR)
  - Ranking: Sentence-Transformers (all-MiniLM-L6-v2)
  - Reasoning: Google Gemini 2.5 Flash

Slide 5: The "Cinematic" Frontend (UI/UX)
- Design Philosophy: "Alive" interface using Organic Backgrounds and Kinetic Typography.
- Interactive Feedback: Magnetic Buttons and Mouse Followers for a premium feel.
- Real-time Visualization: Progress-based "Scanning" animations during analysis.
- Glassmorphism: Clean, transparent layouts with backdrop-blur effects.

Slide 6: OCR Engine - Microsoft Florence-2
- Role: High-fidelity text extraction from complex PDF resumes.
- Model: microsoft/Florence-2-base.
- Task-specific: Uses the <OCR> prompt to parse text while maintaining structural integrity.
- Hardware Optimization: Auto-detects CUDA for GPU acceleration or falls back to CPU.

Slide 7: Semantic Ranking Logic
- Vectorization: Converts Job Descriptions and Resumes into 384-dimensional embeddings.
- Similarity Metric: Uses Cosine Similarity to find the mathematical "closeness" between the JD and the candidate's experience.
- Speed: all-MiniLM-L6-v2 ensures near-instant ranking even for large batches.

Slide 8: LLM Deep Analysis - Gemini 2.5 Flash
- Expert Persona: Acts as a "Digital Hiring Manager".
- Multi-dimensional Scoring: Calculates an "ATS Score" based on nuanced fit rather than just keyword overlap.
- Structured Output: Generates JSON-based summaries, strong matches, and identified gaps.

Slide 9: Interview Question Generation
- Dynamic Tailoring: Questions are not generic; they are generated based on the specific gaps found in the candidate's resume.
- Bridging the Gaps: Helps interviewers focus on areas where the candidate may need further validation.
- Standardized Screening: Ensures every candidate is evaluated against the core requirements of the role.

Slide 10: Implementation Challenges
- PDF Complexity: Handling multi-column layouts and different font encodings during OCR.
- Performance: Balancing heavy AI model loads (Florence-2/Transformers) with a responsive user interface.
- Prompt Engineering: Ensuring consistent JSON outputs from LLMs for reliable frontend rendering.

Slide 11: Deployment & Scalability
- Containerization: Fully Dockerized (backend, frontend, and db services).
- Docker Compose: Single-command deployment (docker-compose up).
- FastAPI Performance: Asynchronous endpoints allow for handling multiple screening requests concurrently.

Slide 12: Future Scope
- Multi-modal Analysis: Analyzing video introductions or portfolio screenshots.
- bias Detection: Automated checks for gender or ethnic bias in AI-generated rankings.
- Integration: APIs for LinkedIn, Indeed, and internal HR management systems.

Slide 13: Conclusion & Q&A
- Summary: The AI Resume Screener transforms recruitment from a manual bottleneck into a data-driven competitive advantage.
